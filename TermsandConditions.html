Condominium Terms and Conditions


Unit Usage and Occupancy

- Each unit is intended for residential purposes only.
- Units may not be used for any commercial or unlawful purposes.

Common Area Usage

- Common areas are to be used by all residents and their guests.
- Residents are responsible for the upkeep and cleanliness of common areas after use.

Maintenance and Repairs

- Unit owners are responsible for the maintenance and repairs of their individual units.
- The association is responsible for maintenance and repairs of common areas and structural components.

Assessment Fees

- Owners are required to pay monthly or annual assessment fees for the upkeep of common areas and services.
- Non-payment of assessment fees may result in late fees, interest, or legal action.

Architectural Changes

- Any proposed modifications or changes to the unit's exterior or common areas must be approved by the association.
- Architectural guidelines and approval processes must be followed.

Pets

- Pets are allowed based on association regulations.
- Owners must adhere to pet-related rules, including leash requirements and waste disposal.

Noise and Nuisance

- Residents are expected to avoid excessive noise that disturbs other residents.
- Activities causing disturbances or nuisances are prohibited.

Guests and Security

- Residents are responsible for the behavior and actions of their guests.
- Security protocols, including key card access, must be followed.

Insurance

- Unit owners are responsible for their individual unit's insurance coverage.
- The association carries insurance for common areas and structures.

Resale and Leasing

- Unit owners may be subject to association approval when reselling or leasing their units.
- The association may have the right of first refusal in some cases.

Dispute Resolution

Disputes between residents or between a resident and the association may
be subject to mediation or arbitration as outlined in the association's bylaws.

Compliance with Laws

- Residents must adhere to all applicable local, state, and federal laws.
- Violation of laws may result in penalties or legal action.
